About Me

I'm a software developer that likes making stupid games as a hobby.  I started off making text adventures in BASIC back in elementary school.  Pretty much every adventure ended in a bear attack.  I'm glad there weren't avenues to easily share those back then, because I would have made a lot of enemies. Once I learned Visual Basic, I finally made a graphical adventure worthy of my storytelling skills.  I don't like to nutshell these things, but basically you played a monkey who had to run under a jumping alien so you could grab the bomb and blow everything, including yourself, up.  My therapist rated it "interesting!"

I wrote a point-and-click adventure game system for XNA back when that was cool, played around with DirectX and OpenGL for a bit, and then largely switched to Unity.  I started this website to post some of my projects for posterity's sake.  It's kind of like a blog, but instead of my ramblings, it's games! 

I like drawing, which lends itself well to making "developer" game art.  The background and logo for this site? All me. I know, that's two whole things!  I also have a regular job writing software that nobody wants to hear about, so that's all I have to say about that.
